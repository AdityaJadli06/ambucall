Emergency First Aid & Basic Life Support

1. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  • Check for responsiveness and breathing.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Place the heel of your hand on the center of the chest and begin compressions.
  • Perform 30 compressions followed by 2 rescue breaths.
  • Continue until help arrives or the person shows signs of life.

2. Choking

  • Ask if the person can cough or speak.
  • If not, stand behind them and give 5 back blows between the shoulder blades.
  • If the object doesn't come out, perform 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Repeat the cycle until the object is expelled or the person becomes unresponsive.

3. Bleeding Control

  • Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth.
  • Elevate the wounded area above the heart if possible.
  • Apply a bandage once bleeding is under control.
  • Seek professional medical help as soon as possible.

4. Burns

  • Cool the burn with running water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Cover with a non-stick, sterile dressing.
  • Do not pop blisters or apply ointments.
  • Seek medical attention for severe burns.
